# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## QA/Testing Lead

### Role Summary
QA/Testing Leads own quality assurance strategy, test planning, and acceptance validation. They collaborate with product and engineering to ensure features meet acceptance criteria and quality standards before release.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain test plans aligned with acceptance criteria
- Lead test case design and execution (manual and automated)
- Identify and triage quality issues and regressions
- Collaborate with developers on testability and test coverage targets
- Validate release readiness through smoke testing and regression suites
- Report quality metrics and trends to PM and Project Manager

### Goals
- Ensure high-quality releases with minimal defects in production
- Reduce time-to-quality through early test planning and automation
- Provide confidence in feature readiness through comprehensive test coverage

### Typical Communication
- Sprint planning and acceptance criteria refinement
- Test status updates in daily standups
- Defect triage and prioritization discussions
- Release validation sign-off before deployment

---

## Technical Lead/Architect

### Role Summary
Technical Leads provide architectural vision and technical direction for projects. They make key design decisions, guide developers on implementation approaches, and help identify and mitigate technical risks.

### Responsibilities
- Define technical architecture and design patterns for the project
- Guide developers on implementation strategies and best practices
- Conduct technical design reviews and architecture decisions
- Identify and propose solutions to technical risks and dependencies
- Mentor junior developers and support knowledge sharing
- Ensure alignment between project design and system scalability goals

### Goals
- Deliver technically sound, maintainable, and scalable solutions
- Reduce rework and technical debt through thoughtful design decisions
- Build team technical capability and knowledge

### Typical Communication
- Technical design discussions and architecture reviews
- Code review guidance and mentoring
- Risk identification and technical trade-off discussions
- Integration planning with other system components

---

## Scrum Master/Agile Coach

### Role Summary
Scrum Masters facilitate agile ceremonies, remove team blockers, and coach teams on agile practices. They enable the team to self-organize and continuously improve delivery velocity and team health.

### Responsibilities
- Facilitate sprint planning, daily standups, reviews, and retrospectives
- Identify and help resolve team blockers and dependencies
- Coach team on agile practices and continuous improvement
- Maintain sprint board and artifacts (backlog, burndown)
- Track team velocity and flag capacity concerns
- Escalate organizational impediments blocking team progress

### Goals
- Maximize team velocity and delivery predictability
- Create psychological safety and continuous improvement culture
- Remove obstacles to team productivity

### Typical Communication
- Facilitation of all agile ceremonies
- One-on-ones with team members on concerns
- Escalation communications to PM and leadership
- Retrospective action item tracking

---

## Stakeholder/Sponsor

### Role Summary
Stakeholders and Sponsors provide business context, executive support, and key approvals for projects. They represent customer and business needs, make go/no-go decisions, and help remove cross-organizational blockers.

### Responsibilities
- Define business goals and success metrics for the project
- Provide executive sponsorship and resource commitment
- Approve key project decisions and trade-offs
- Communicate project importance and status across the organization
- Remove cross-functional and organizational blockers
- Validate that solutions meet business needs

### Goals
- Ensure projects deliver measurable business value
- Maintain alignment between delivery and business strategy
- Enable project success through organizational support and resources

### Typical Communication
- Project initiation and kickoff alignment
- Monthly or milestone-based stakeholder updates
- Escalation handling for business-impacting issues
- Decision gates and approval discussions

---

## Operations/DevOps Engineer

### Role Summary
Operations and DevOps Engineers ensure infrastructure readiness, deployment automation, and production support. They partner with development teams to enable reliable, scalable releases.

### Responsibilities
- Design and maintain deployment pipelines and CI/CD infrastructure
- Plan and execute deployments to production
- Monitor production systems and respond to operational incidents
- Collaborate with developers on infrastructure requirements and scaling
- Maintain system observability, logging, and alerting
- Support incident response and post-incident analysis

### Goals
- Enable fast, reliable, repeatable deployments with minimal downtime
- Maintain high system availability and performance
- Reduce deployment risk through automation and monitoring

### Typical Communication
- Release planning and deployment window coordination
- Infrastructure requirements discussions during project planning
- Incident response and status updates
- Production monitoring and performance metrics sharing
